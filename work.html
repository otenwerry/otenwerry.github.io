Work

I’m a technical AI alignment researcher. Right now, I’m researching long-context alignment drift in LLM agents, mentored by Maksym Andriushchenko as part of the MATS 10.0 cohort. Before MATS, I researched out-of-distribution propensity generalization in LLMs, mentored by Niels Warncke through SPAR. See Google Scholar and LessWrong for more.

AI systems are becoming able to execute increasingly complex tasks over long time horizons with less and less supervision. If this trend continues for long enough, major safety challenges could emerge: how do we know that the AI is doing what we think it’s doing, if it’s working on something really complicated? How do we know if it’s engaging in something like self-reflection, changing its own characteristics over time? My current research interests revolve around these sorts of questions: understanding the extent to which these problems are real, and looking for ways to increase our confidence that increasingly agentic AIs embody values that remain aligned with the interests of humanity.

In the past, I’ve done independent AI alignment research in the ValuesLab, AI safety grantmaking at the Effective Institutions Project, and climate science research at Columbia’s earth observatory. During undergrad, I built Lion Dine and Swipe Market, both of which are still being used by a lot of Columbia students, as far as I know. Lion Dine is now owned by Spectator Publishing Company, and Swipe Market by Columbia Software Solutions.
